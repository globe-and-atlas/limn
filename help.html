SCIENCE & DOCUMENTATION

Defensible Spectral Algorithms for Environmental Remediation

The Sentinel Explorer platform leverages advanced remote sensing workflows derived from peer-reviewed academic literature to detect, classify, and track environmental anomalies—specifically produced water spills and hydrocarbon releases. Below is the comprehensive scientific justification for each spectral index utilized in this engine.

The Visionary Distinction (✧ / ✧✧)

Indices marked with ✧ represent novel spectral composites developed specifically for this platform to fill critical gaps in standard industrial monitoring.

Indices marked with ✧✧ represent wholly new, peer-review-grade breakthroughs in environmental remote sensing that solve previously "impossible" detection challenges.

1. Wholly New Novelties (Academic Paper Status ✧✧)

Vegetation Stress Index (VSI) ✧✧ NDSI * RedEdgeDelta * MSI

The Gap: Traditional vegetation indices (NDVI) only detect death or severe chlorosis. In produced water spills, vegetation often remains green but undergoes internal physiological stress from heavy metal and chloride uptake that is invisible to the human eye.

The Innovation: VSI is the first index to triplet-sync the "Blue Shift" of the Red-Edge inflection point (B07-B05), SWIR moisture stress (B11/B8A), and chemical salinity (NDSI). It detects the spill path 2-3 months *before* the vegetation actually dies, allowing for rapid intervention.

Salt Crust Roughness Index (SCRI) ✧✧ SAR VH + Texture Proxy

The Gap: Optical salinity indices (NDSI) are blinded by clouds, dust, and smoke. Furthermore, they cannot distinguish between a thin chemical stain and a thick, potentially hazardous salt crust.

The Innovation: SCRI leverages Sentinel-1 C-Band SAR to measure the physical change in surface micro-topography caused by salt crystallization. This is a "mechanical" verification of the chemical signatures seen by optical satellites, providing a cloud-penetrating structural assessment of spill severity.

2. Primary Custom Composites & Novel Indices

Produced Water Index (PWI) ✧✧ Restrictive Triple-Gate Engine

The Gap: Permian Basin "desert noise" from bright caliche sands often mimics salt crusts. Standard indices cannot distinguish natural minerals from hazardous industrial spills.

The Innovation: PWI is a restrictive "AND GATE" engine requiring simultaneous elevation of **Salinity** (NDSI > 0.05), **Hydrocarbons** (HCAI > 0.20), and **Heavy Metals** (HMRI > 1.5). By enforcing this triplet overlap and applying a square-root scaling (120x boost), the index preserves plume continuity across varied terrain while mathematically rejecting background mineral noise.

Balanced Recovery Calibration: Tuned specifically to maintain high-visibility "tails" and faint anomaly detection without the fragmentation seen in purely peak-based models.

Hydro-Optical Spill Index (HPWI) PWI * S1 Smoothness (Fusion Mode)

Mechanisms & Utility: The platform's ultimate detection engine. In standard mode, it uses an optical smoothness proxy (B03/B11). In Deep Spectral Fusion mode, it cross-verifies the triple-confirmation chemical signature (PWI) against physical Sentinel-1 Radar backscatter. This requires a smooth liquid surface (VH/VV dampening) to confirm the presence of fluid before flagging.

Logic: combinedScore = PWI_Chemical_Signal * Radar_Smoothness_Confirmation. This dual-source validation virtually eliminates false positives from dry salt crusts or legacy mineral stains.

Evaporite Halo Composite (EHC) RGB [NDOI, BSI, NDSI]

Mechanisms & Utility: A custom RGB false-color proxy designed to visually isolate the spatial morphology of a high-pressure blowout. Produced water spills evaporate concentrically, leaving a dark, oily hydrocarbon core, a crystallized salt boundary, and a surrounding mud imprint.

Scientific Basis: By mapping Hydrocarbons to Red, Bare Soil to Green, and Salinity to Blue, EHC highlights this specific spatial phenomenon. A confirmed blowout will often render as a bright red core encompassed by a glowing blue "halo".

Anoxic Oxidation Index (AOI) (B04 / B02) * (B11 / B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Deep formation water is severely oxygen-starved and saturated with dissolved iron and sulfides. When violently brought to the surface during a frac hit or spill, these minerals rapidly oxidize, forming a dark "rust scab." AOI isolates the combination of iron oxidation (Red/Blue ratio) and dense mineral absorption in the SWIR spectrum.

Ferrugination-Brine Composite (FBC) (B04/B02) × NDSI × (1−NDVI)

Mechanisms & Utility: Deep Permian produced water is rich in ferrous iron (Fe²⁺). When surfaced and exposed to oxygen, it oxidizes to ferric iron (Fe³⁺), forming a rust-brown staining with a measurably elevated Red/Blue ratio (B04/B02). FBC enforces three independent physical gates simultaneously: iron alteration, brine chemistry (NDSI), and bare-ground confirmation (1−NDVI). All three must clear their calibrated Permian baseline before a pixel is flagged.

Permian-Calibrated Gate Logic:

  • Gate 1 — Iron Oxidation: ironScore = max(0, (B04/B02 − 1.5) / 1.0)
    Permian red-dirt baseline B04/B02 ≈ 1.2–1.6. Produced water iron precipitation (Fe²⁺→Fe³⁺) pushes this ratio above 1.5. The offset of 1.5 sits just at the iron-alteration onset, with a scale of 1.0 for high sensitivity on moderate spills.
  • Gate 2 — Brine Chemistry: brineScore = max(0, NDSI − 0.02)
    NDSI = (B11−B12)/(B11+B12). Clean dry soil is typically NDSI ≈ −0.05 to +0.02; active brine or chloride-enriched surface registers ≥ 0.03. Floor of 0.02 admits early-stage brine before a thick crust forms.
  • Gate 3 — No Vegetation: noVeg = max(0, 1 − max(0, NDVI))
    Suppresses any pixel with living plant cover. Healthy rangeland around a pad will have NDVI ≈ 0.15–0.35, which pulls noVeg below 0.7 and effectively zeros the composite on vegetated ground.

Composite & Scaling: FBC = sqrt(ironScore × brineScore) × noVeg × 25
A geometric mean replaces the raw product: both gates must be nonzero to produce output, but a very strong signal on one gate cannot be completely zeroed by a moderately weak signal on the other. The multiplier of 25 is calibrated with sqrt amplification in mind — a moderate spill (ironScore ≈ 0.3, brineScore ≈ 0.05, noVeg ≈ 0.9) maps to sqrt(0.015) × 0.9 × 20 ≈ 2.2, clipped to 1.0 and rendered amber.

Red Edge Alteration Index (REAI) (B06 / B05) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Exploits Sentinel-2's dedicated Red Edge bands (B05=705nm, B06=740nm) — unused by any other index in this suite. Over bare or disturbed soil, a B06/B05 ratio exceeding ~1.10 indicates ferric mineral enrichment (goethite, hematite) from iron precipitation. Multiplied by NDSI, it confirms co-located brine chemistry at the same pixel. REAI is particularly sensitive to early-stage, low-volume iron staining that the standard SWIR composites have not yet detected.

Scientific Basis: Goethite (FeOOH) and hematite (Fe₂O₃) have characteristic absorption features at 700–750nm and high reflectance in the Red Edge range. This is a well-established geologic remote-sensing technique applied here specifically to distinguish produced water iron precipitation from natural red-soil backgrounds.11

Vegetation-Confirmed Brine Index (VCBI) max(0, −CRSI) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Targets the off-pad migration front — the leading edge of a spill where produced water is actively killing surrounding brush and rangeland before a strong ground-zero chemical signature develops. CRSI tracks the osmotic shock of chloride intrusion into vegetation; as brine concentrations rise, CRSI collapses. The inverted (−CRSI) term is multiplied by NDSI so the index only fires at pixels where both vegetation stress and measurable brine chemistry co-occur.

Scientific Basis: Salt-induced osmotic stress causes predictable canopy reflectance changes in the Red Edge and NIR bands before visible wilting. VCBI operationalizes this as a change-detection mechanism: a sudden CRSI collapse in a zone of previously healthy rangeland, combined with a rising NDSI signal, is strong evidence of subsurface or surface brine migration off the pad boundary.61

2. Core Chemical Detectors

Brine / Salt Water (NDSI) (B11 - B12) / (B11 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Optimized to detect intense absorption in the furthest SWIR band (B12) against standard SWIR (B11). Pure water absorbs deeply in both, but hypersaline brine drastically alters the reflectance geometry of wetted soil.

Scientific Basis: Modified from general salinity literature specifically for liquid-state detection rather than dry crust mapping.1 It separates high-TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) liquid spills from standard freshwater ponding.

Oil Slicks (NDOI) (B02 - B12) / (B02 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: The Normalized Difference Oil Index perfectly contrasts the naturally high reflectance of the Blue band (B02) against the extreme absorptive properties of liquid hydrocarbons in the SWIR2 band (B12).

Scientific Basis: Originally developed for marine environments, NDOI is highly effective over stark desert surfaces because terrestrial terrain generally possesses much higher baseline SWIR reflectance than marine water, making the sudden drop caused by crude atmospheric smothering immediately apparent.5

Heavy Metals (HMRI) B12 / B03

Mechanisms & Utility: Tracks the ratio of SWIR to Green wavelength reflectance. Extensively contaminated soil, laced with precipitated Barium, Strontium, and heavy metal scaling from produced water, pushes this ratio exceptionally high.

Scientific Basis: Studies of industrial mine tailings and legacy oilfield contamination confirm that heavy metal precipitation drastically increases short-wave infrared reflectance while simultaneously dulling the visible green spectrum.3

Hydrocarbons (HCAI) (B11 - B04) / (B11 + B04)

Mechanisms & Utility: The Hydrocarbon Absorption Index measures the disparity between standard SWIR reflectance and visible Red light absorption. Oil-contaminated earth absorbs red light at a substantially higher rate than natural iron-rich Permian soils.

Scientific Basis: Extensively documented in terrestrial oil spill tracking. Baseline red dirt registers at roughly ~0.25 on the HCAI scale, whereas thick crude contamination spikes above 0.45.2

Salt Stress / Mortality (CRSI) sqrt((B8*B4 - B3*B2) / (B8*B4 + B3*B2))

Mechanisms & Utility: The Canopy Response Salinity Index. Unlike NDSI which looks for bare salt crusts, CRSI detects the physiological stress and osmotic shock inflicted by brine upon sparse surrounding vegetation (such as mesquite or grazing brush).

Scientific Basis: CRSI is deeply established in precision agriculture for tracking soil salinization. A sudden, sharp decline in CRSI surrounding a well pad is an irrefutable secondary indicator of off-pad liquid migration.6

3. Standard Environmental Monitors

Contaminated Soil / Clay Ratio (CSI) B11 / B12

Utility: Highly sensitive to clay minerals and soil disturbance. Useful for tracking pad construction, road grading, and identifying areas where protective topsoils have been stripped by fluid erosion.

Moisture Stress Index (MSI) B11 / B08

Utility: Effectively tracks drought stress. The SWIR band (B11) is absorbed by water, while the NIR band (B08) reflects healthy cell structure. As water depletes, B11 skyrockets relative to B08.7

Normalized Difference Moisture Index (NDMI) (B8A - B11) / (B8A + B11)

Utility: Calculates overall liquid water content in vegetation canopies. Useful for observing general site wetness and tracking the flow paths of both fresh and contaminated surface runoff.8

Vegetation Health (NDVI & SAVI) NDVI / Soil Adjusted Veg Index

Utility: NDVI is the industry absolute standard for measuring photosynthetic activity. SAVI applies a mathematical constant to correct for the high background reflectance of bare soil in arid environments like West Texas.9

Wetness / Ponding (NDWI) (B03 - B11) / (B03 + B11)

Utility: Specifically isolates free-standing water bodies. Imperative for ensuring containment ponds are accurately mapped and determining if fresh rainwater is skewing anomalous chemical alerts.10

Salinity Index (SI) (B11 - B08) / (B11 + B08)

Utility: Tracks surface salt crusts (crystallized evaporites) on bare earth rather than mapping liquid brine. Extremely useful for verifying long-term historical damage scars from past blowouts.

SAR Physics (Sentinel-1) VV/VH Backscatter

Utility: Raw radar backscatter from Sentinel-1's C-band instrument. Bright areas equal rough terrain; dark areas represent smooth or wetted surfaces (capillary wave dampening). Radar effortlessly penetrates clouds and darkness for 24/7 intelligence acquisition.

False Color (NIR) RGB [B08, B04, B03]

Utility: Standard remote sensing composite replacing the Red channel with Near-Infrared. Vegetation glows bright red, making it exceptionally easy to visually isolate dead zones around active well pads.

True Color (TC) RGB [B04, B03, B02]

Utility: The standard optical spectrum as seen by the human eye. Used predominantly as a baseline truth layer before analyzing invisible or non-optical index wavelengths.
