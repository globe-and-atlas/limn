Defensible Spectral Algorithms for Environmental Remediation — Validated 2026-03-28

The Sentinel Explorer platform leverages advanced remote sensing workflows derived from peer-reviewed academic literature to detect, classify, and track environmental anomalies—specifically produced water spills and hydrocarbon releases. Below is the comprehensive scientific justification for each spectral index utilized in this engine.

Temporal Relevance Guide

Spectral signatures evolve as produced water interacts with the environment. Use the badges below to select the right tool for the age of the spill:

Live / 0-1M - Detects active wetting and dynamic moisture. 0-3M - Optimal window for liquid chemical signatures. 3-12M - Detects iron oxidation and early salt crusting. 6M+ - Forensic detection of legacy residues and mineral scabs. Persistent - Detects long-term soil/geological structural changes.

Permian Environmental Optimization (V40+)

The Permian Basin presents unique spectral challenges: high background reflectance from caliche soils, bright gypsum beds, and extremely low humidity. The following adjustments are hard-coded into the primary detection suites to suppress these natural "Project 0" false positives:

  • The "Liquid Gate" (+0.5 NDWI): Arid rangelands have a baseline NDWI near -0.6. We apply a +0.5 offset to lift "damp soil" signatures into the positive detection range without firing on bone-dry caliche.
  • BSI Mask Relaxation (-0.3): Standard soil masks (BSI) reject water bodies (negative BSI). Because severe hypersaline spills and standing oil appear spectrally "smooth" like water, we lowered the mask to -0.3 to prevent high-severity detections from being clipped.
  • SWIR Triple-Lock Offsets: PWCI (formerly PWI) enforces calibrated offsets — Salinity (NDSI floor: 0.03), Hydrocarbons (HCAI floor: 0.05), and Heavy Metals (HMRI floor: 1.1) — tuned against confirmed spill records to sit just above the Permian ambient mineral baseline. Original thresholds (0.10 / 0.30 / 2.0) produced 0% detection; the current values yield 81.5% on TRRC sites.
  • Dry Brine Mode (ASAI + OBEC): Permian Basin bare caliche has NDWI = −0.39 to −0.51 (B11 >> B03), which zeros the wet-mode optical smoothness proxy in both ASAI (formerly PWOI) and OBEC (formerly HPWI). A parallel dry path fires when NDWI < −0.30 AND NDSI > 0.05 AND BSI > 0.10. The result is max(wet_score, dry_score) — preserving all liquid detections while recovering dry/evaporated salt crust sites.

1. Validated Produced Water Composites

Original custom composite architectures and Permian Basin calibrations by Daniel Bally (Globe & Atlas, 2025–2026). Standard spectral indicators have full literature citations in Section 3; custom G&A composites are marked as follows to highlight intellectual ownership and original engineering:
✧✧ Globe & Atlas Original Composite: A completely original, highly novel composite architecture featuring custom multi-gate logic, desert-specific calibrations, or multi-sensor specular proxies with no published prior equivalent.
✧ Globe & Atlas Calibrated Composite: An original composite assembly and target-specific calibration of established band ratios, purpose-built for Permian Basin oilfield geochemistry.

ASAI — Arid Salinity Anomaly Index (formerly PWOI / APEX) ✧✧ Live / Deep Fusion Optical Smoothness × Moisture+Brine → SAR Coincidence (Deep)

The Gap: Identifying industrial spills in complex terrain produces "look-alike" false positives from shadows, bright soil, or dry salt ponds. No single-source optical index can reliably separate chemical moisture from physical surface texture across varied lighting and weather conditions.

The Implementation: ASAI (formerly PWOI) is the platform's broad-spectrum anomaly net. It uses a surface smoothness proxy — the ratio (B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11), an NDWI-like value that mirrors how specularly smooth liquid surfaces suppress SAR VH backscatter — cross-referenced against a combined moisture+brine score. A pixel is only flagged if it simultaneously exhibits optical smoothness and elevated chemical moisture, with a non-linear boost for high-confidence coincidence. In Deep Fusion mode, the optical smoothness proxy is replaced with true Sentinel-1 SAR VH backscatter, achieving genuine radar-optical coincidence detection. Formerly known as Produced Water Optical Index (PWOI) or APEX Anomaly Index.41831

Dry Brine Mode: The wet-mode formula zeros on dry Permian Basin bare soil (NDWI = −0.39 to −0.51), which was the root cause of pre-fix 29.6% detection. A parallel dry path — (NDSI − 0.04) × min(1, BSI × N) × scale — fires when NDWI < −0.30 AND NDSI > 0.05 AND BSI > 0.10, recovering evaporated salt crust signatures. Score takes max(wet, dry) to preserve standing-water detections.

Validation (2026-03-28): 77.8% detection on 27 TRRC confirmed spill sites; 87.5% on 8 GPS-verified sourced sites; 86% on major spills (>500 BBL). Pre-dry-brine-mode baseline was 29.6%.

ASAI vs. OBEC — When to Use Each

ASAI and OBEC produce visually similar outputs because both use the B03/B11 optical smoothness ratio in standard mode. The critical difference is in what chemical evidence they require before firing:

Role Broad anomaly net — early warning Confirmation engine — high specificity
Chemical gate Optical smoothness × (NDOI + brine boost); dry mode: NDSI × BSI product when NDWI < −0.30 NDOI × norm_smooth composite; dry mode: same NDSI × BSI parallel path; both take max(wet, dry)
Sensitivity vs. specificity Higher sensitivity — catches weaker and fresher anomalies Higher specificity — fewer detections, but more confirmed
What it fires on that OBEC doesn't Fresh water, mild brine, early-stage spills where the full chemical triplet hasn't developed —
What OBEC fires on that ASAI doesn't — Mature spills with full triple-lock chemistry confirmed
Deep Fusion mode True SAR VH coincidence with optical moisture proxy Triple-gate chemical signal × true SAR VH smoothness
Recommended workflow Run first to identify candidate areas Run second to confirm ASAI detections as produced water
False positives include Irrigation, ponded rainwater, natural seeps Near-zero in Deep Fusion mode

Practical workflow: Use ASAI to scan the AOI for anything anomalous — any optically smooth, chemically active pixel. Then switch to OBEC (or OBEC Deep Fusion) on candidate sites to confirm whether the chemistry qualifies as produced water. A detection that appears on ASAI but not OBEC is worth investigating as a fresh or mild anomaly. A detection that appears on both is a high-confidence spill indicator.

PWCI — Produced Water Chemical Index (formerly PWI) ✧✧ 0-3M Restrictive Triple-Gate Engine

The Gap: Permian Basin "desert noise" from bright caliche sands often mimics salt crusts. Standard indices cannot distinguish natural minerals from hazardous industrial spills because they test for only one chemical signature at a time.

The Implementation: PWCI (formerly PWI) is a restrictive "AND GATE" engine requiring the simultaneous elevation of three independent chemical signatures: Salinity (NDSI floor: 0.03), Hydrocarbons (HCAI floor: 0.05, scale ×5), and Heavy Metals (HMRI floor: 1.1, scale ×3). The Sentinel Explorer implementation applies all three simultaneously as a triple-lock composite for oilfield produced water. The multiplicative gating means a strong signal on one component cannot compensate for a missing signal on another — all three must be present. A power scaling (pow(pwi_base × 50, 1.2)) amplifies confirmed detections while suppressing marginal signals. Formerly known as Produced Water Index (PWI).1222

Threshold Calibration Note: Original thresholds (NDSI 0.10, HCAI 0.30, HMRI 2.0 — standard literature values) produced 0% detection on 32 TRRC sites in the 2026-03-08 baseline run. Root cause: TRRC parcel centroid coordinates offset the sample bbox over mixed caliche + vegetation pixels, depressing all three signals simultaneously. Thresholds were lowered and the hard BSI gate (if bsi <= 0.01 → pwi = 0) was replaced with a soft weight (floor 0.3) to tolerate centroid-offset sampling.

Validation (2026-03-28): 81.5% detection on 27 TRRC confirmed spill sites (threshold 0.01), mean score 0.741. ASAI detects major spills (>500 BBL) at 86%. The triple-gate AND architecture is now substantiated against a real confirmed-spill dataset.

2. Custom Composites & Applied Indices

Original composite indices by Daniel Bally (2025–2026). These combine established spectral band physics in novel architectures calibrated for produced water detection across varied spill states — active liquid, dry evaporite, and legacy residue.

OBEC — Oil-Brine Emulsion Composite (formerly HPWI) ✧ 0-3M PWCI × Smoothness (Optical / SAR)

Mechanisms & Utility: The platform's highest-precision confirmation engine. In standard mode, OBEC (formerly HPWI) combines an optical blue/SWIR contrast index (NDOI: (B02 − B12) / (B02 + B12)) sensitive to dissolved mineral ions in brine, a brine boost from NDSI, and the surface smoothness proxy ((B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11)) as a liquid-surface gate. In Deep Spectral Fusion mode, the optical proxy is replaced by true Sentinel-1 SAR VH backscatter, achieving genuine dual-source confirmation: a pixel must simultaneously carry the chemical fingerprint of produced water and the physical surface smoothness of standing fluid. Formerly known as Hybrid Produced Water Index (HPWI).41731

Dry Brine Mode: Same parallel path as ASAI — fires when NDWI < −0.30 AND NDSI > 0.05 AND BSI > 0.10. Formula: (NDSI − 0.04) × min(1, BSI × 3.5) × 14. Score takes max(wet, dry). This recovered the 52 percentage-point detection gap on dry evaporated brine sites.

Validation (2026-03-28): 66.7% on 27 TRRC sites. Pre-dry-brine-mode baseline was 14.8%. See the ASAI entry above for a direct ASAI vs. OBEC workflow comparison.

Liquid Brine Index (LBI) ✧ 0-3M NDSI × NDWI × (1 − NDVI) × BSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Captures actively standing pools of hazardous produced water rather than legacy residues. LBI enforces four simultaneous gates: a brine chemical signature (NDSI), a standing water proxy (NDWI), absence of vegetation (1−NDVI), and bare-soil confirmation (BSI). The four-way product means the index is nearly silent on dry salt flats, clean ponded rainwater, and vegetated areas — it fires specifically at bare ground with co-located liquid brine chemistry.

Calibration: The NDWI and BSI offsets are Permian-adjusted (standard preset: NDWI +0.0, BSI +0.0; arid/Permian preset: NDWI +0.5, BSI −0.3) to account for the extreme dry baseline of West Texas soils, which would otherwise suppress valid detections.

Scientific Basis: Built from established salinity and water-index literature, assembled specifically for the liquid brine detection use case not addressed by any single index.110

Vegetation Stress Index (VSI) ✧ 3-24M NDSI × RedEdgeDelta × MSI

The Gap: Traditional vegetation indices (NDVI) only detect death or severe chlorosis. In produced water spills, vegetation often remains visibly green while undergoing internal physiological stress from heavy metal and chloride uptake — damage invisible to standard optical sensors.

The Application: VSI combines the "Blue Shift" of the Red-Edge inflection point ((B07 − B05) / (B07 + B05)) — indicative of chlorophyll disruption from chloride toxicity — with SWIR moisture stress (B11 / B8A) and chemical salinity (NDSI). The triplet combination is calibrated to detect physiological stress in Permian rangeland vegetation 2–3 months before visible wilting.61520

Attribution note: Vegetation stress indices and red-edge salinity-stress methods are well-documented in precision agriculture (Allbed 2014, Scudiero 2014). The Sentinel Explorer contribution is the local triplet assembly and calibration for the oilfield produced water context, not the underlying band physics or the general VSI concept.

Salt Crust Roughness Index (SCRI) ✧ 3M+ SAR VH Backscatter + VH/VV Ratio

The Gap: Optical salinity indices (NDSI) are blinded by clouds, dust, and smoke, and cannot distinguish between a thin chemical stain and a thick, potentially hazardous salt crust.

The Application: SCRI uses Sentinel-1 C-Band SAR backscatter to detect surface-state changes caused by salt crystallization. Dry, rough salt crusts produce higher VH backscatter than smooth liquid surfaces; the combination of absolute VH amplitude and the VH/VV cross-polarization ratio provides a cloud-penetrating proxy for salt crust presence and relative thickness.173031

Implementation note: The current formula uses log-scaled VH amplitude and a VH−VV ratio — standard SAR backscatter intensity analysis. It is not a full spatial texture measure (no GLCM or second-order statistics). The "roughness" claim is a backscatter-intensity proxy, not a true surface micro-topography measurement. A future version using windowed texture statistics would strengthen the physical justification.

Brine-Pavement Index (BPI) ✧ Live NDSI × HCAI × BSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Optimized for detecting leaks on caliche pads and lease roads — the most common spill scenario at the wellhead level. BPI enforces three gates simultaneously: brine chemistry (NDSI), hydrocarbon absorption (HCAI), and bare/compacted soil confirmation (BSI). This is the primary tool for pad-level integrity monitoring, where the surface is already stripped of vegetation and the relevant chemistry is brine + crude co-contamination on compacted ground.

Calibration: The BSI offset (−0.3 in Permian mode) is critical: hard caliche pads are spectrally similar to dry playas, and without the offset, legitimate pad detections would be clipped by a strict BSI floor. The NDSI floor of 0.03 and HCAI floor of 0.15 are set just above the Permian ambient mineral baseline.12

Toxic Residue Index (TRI) ✧ 6M+ NDSI × HMRI × AOI

Mechanisms & Utility: A forensic tool for identifying historical spill footprints where liquid is no longer present. TRI combines three post-evaporation residue signatures: salt chemistry (NDSI), heavy metal precipitation (HMRI — the B12/B03 ratio elevated by Barium, Strontium, and iron scaling), and anoxic oxidation (AOI — the iron-rust "scab" from Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺ oxidation). All three must clear calibrated Permian baselines (NDSI > 0.05, HMRI > 1.5, AOI > 1.5) before the pixel fires.

Use case: The 6M+ temporal window reflects the time required for heavy metal precipitation and iron oxidation to accumulate to detectable levels after liquid has evaporated. TRI is the primary tool for identifying "cold" legacy contamination from spills that occurred before the available satellite record.31122

Petro-Hydrocarbon Index (PHI) ✧ 0-6M NDSI × (B11/B12) × HCAI

Mechanisms & Utility: Isolates oily brine specifically — distinguishing co-contaminated produced water from clean runoff or pure salt. PHI gates on three co-occurring signals: salinity (NDSI), the SWIR absorption shoulder elevation indicative of petroleum hydrocarbons (B11/B12 > 1.0), and the hydrocarbon absorption index (HCAI > 0.20). Clean water and dry salt both fail the shoulder + HCAI combination; crude-contaminated brine elevates all three simultaneously.213

Clay-Mineral Alteration (CMA) ✧ Persistent NDSI × (B11/B12) × (B04/B02)

Mechanisms & Utility: A forensic index targeting the chemical modification of clay lattices by produced water residues. Highly saline formation water (TDS >100,000 mg/L) replaces exchangeable cations in clay minerals, shifting the SWIR-1/SWIR-2 ratio. Iron-oxide co-precipitation from the same brine elevates the Red/Blue visible ratio. CMA requires both the SWIR clay-interaction signal and the iron-oxidation signal to be co-located with a salt baseline (NDSI > 0), making it specific to brine-altered, iron-enriched soil chemistry rather than simple dust or caliche.

Temporal window: Clay lattice cation exchange and iron precipitate accumulation are slow processes — CMA is most useful 6+ months post-spill and can persist for years, making it complementary to TRI for mapping the permanent footprint of legacy contamination.11127

Heavy Metal Interaction (HMI) ✧ 12M+ (B03/B02) × (B11/B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Targets Barium and Strontium proxies — the dominant dissolved solids in Permian produced water. Heavy metal precipitation shifts the Green/Blue visible ratio (B03/B02) as metal sulfates and carbonates alter surface spectral properties. The mineral salt precipitation signature (B11/B12 > 1.2) confirms co-located salt-mineral deposition. Both gates use offsets calibrated to sit above the Permian ambient baseline.

Comparison to HMRI: HMRI (B12/B03) is a simpler ratio with broader detection. HMI is a multiplicative composite requiring co-location of both the green-shift and the SWIR mineral signature — more specific to the brine-heavy-metal interaction rather than general SWIR brightness.325

Evaporite Halo Composite (EHC) ✧ 0-3M RGB [NDOI, BSI, NDSI]

Mechanisms & Utility: A custom RGB false-color proxy designed to visually isolate the spatial morphology of a high-pressure blowout. Produced water spills evaporate concentrically, leaving a dark oily hydrocarbon core, a crystallized salt boundary, and a surrounding mud imprint.

Scientific Basis: By mapping Hydrocarbons to Red, Bare Soil to Green, and Salinity to Blue, EHC highlights this specific spatial phenomenon. A confirmed blowout will often render as a bright red core encompassed by a glowing blue "halo".141926

Anoxic Oxidation Index (AOI) ✧ 0-6M (B04/B02) × (B11/B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Deep formation water is severely oxygen-starved and saturated with dissolved iron and sulfides. When brought to the surface during a frac hit or spill, these minerals rapidly oxidize, forming a dark "rust scab." AOI isolates the combination of iron oxidation (Red/Blue ratio) and dense mineral absorption in the SWIR spectrum.112227

Ferrugination-Brine Composite (FBC) ✧ 3-12M (B04/B02) × NDSI × (1 − NDVI)

Mechanisms & Utility: Deep Permian produced water is rich in ferrous iron (Fe²⁺). When surfaced and exposed to oxygen, it oxidizes to ferric iron (Fe³⁺), forming a rust-brown staining with a measurably elevated Red/Blue ratio (B04/B02). FBC enforces three independent physical gates simultaneously: iron alteration, brine chemistry (NDSI), and bare-ground confirmation (1−NDVI).

Permian-Calibrated Gate Logic:

  • Gate 1 — Iron Oxidation: ironScore = max(0, (B04/B02 − 1.5) / 1.0)
    Permian red-dirt baseline B04/B02 ≈ 1.2–1.6. Iron precipitation pushes above 1.5.
  • Gate 2 — Brine Chemistry: brineScore = max(0, NDSI − 0.02)
    Floor of 0.02 admits early-stage brine before a thick crust forms.
  • Gate 3 — No Vegetation: noVeg = max(0, 1 − max(0, NDVI))
    Suppresses any pixel with living plant cover.

Composite & Scaling: FBC = sqrt(ironScore × brineScore) × noVeg × 25
A geometric mean replaces the raw product so both iron and brine gates must be nonzero, but a very strong signal on one cannot be completely zeroed by a moderately weak signal on the other.

Scientific Basis:11121819 Ferric iron (goethite/hematite) has a characteristic absorption feature at 700–750 nm and elevated Red-band reflectance — a well-established geologic remote-sensing signature applied here to distinguish produced water iron precipitation from the native Permian red-soil background.

Red Edge Alteration Index (REAI) ✧ 3-12M (B06/B05) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Exploits Sentinel-2's dedicated Red Edge bands (B05=705nm, B06=740nm) — unused by any other index in this suite. Over bare or disturbed soil, a B06/B05 ratio exceeding ~1.10 indicates ferric mineral enrichment (goethite, hematite) from iron precipitation. Multiplied by NDSI, it confirms co-located brine chemistry at the same pixel. REAI is particularly sensitive to early-stage, low-volume iron staining that the SWIR composites have not yet detected.

Scientific Basis: Goethite (FeOOH) and hematite (Fe₂O₃) have characteristic absorption features at 700–750nm and high reflectance in the Red Edge range. This is a well-established geologic remote-sensing technique applied here to distinguish produced water iron precipitation from natural red-soil backgrounds.11

Vegetation-Confirmed Brine Index (VCBI) ✧ 6-24M max(0, −CRSI) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: Targets the off-pad migration front — the leading edge of a spill where produced water is actively killing surrounding brush and rangeland before a strong ground-zero chemical signature develops. CRSI tracks the osmotic shock of chloride intrusion; as brine concentrations rise, CRSI collapses. The inverted (−CRSI) term is multiplied by NDSI so the index only fires at pixels where both vegetation stress and measurable brine chemistry co-occur.

Scientific Basis: Salt-induced osmotic stress causes predictable canopy reflectance changes in the Red Edge and NIR bands before visible wilting. VCBI operationalizes this as a change-detection mechanism: a sudden CRSI collapse in a zone of previously healthy rangeland, combined with a rising NDSI signal, is strong evidence of subsurface or surface brine migration off the pad boundary.61

3. Core Chemical Detectors

Brine / Salt Water (NDSI) 0-12M (B11 − B12) / (B11 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Optimized to detect intense absorption in the furthest SWIR band (B12) against standard SWIR (B11). Pure water absorbs deeply in both, but hypersaline brine drastically alters the reflectance geometry of wetted soil.

Scientific Basis: Modified from general salinity literature specifically for liquid-state detection rather than dry crust mapping.1 Separates high-TDS liquid spills from standard freshwater ponding.

Oil Slicks (NDOI) 0-3M (B02 − B12) / (B02 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: The Normalized Difference Oil Index contrasts the naturally high reflectance of the Blue band (B02) against the extreme absorptive properties of liquid hydrocarbons in the SWIR2 band (B12).

Scientific Basis: Originally developed for marine environments, NDOI is highly effective over stark desert surfaces because terrestrial terrain has much higher baseline SWIR reflectance than marine water, making the sudden drop caused by crude atmospheric smothering immediately apparent.5

Heavy Metals (HMRI) 12M+ B12 / B03

Mechanisms & Utility: Tracks the ratio of SWIR to Green wavelength reflectance. Extensively contaminated soil, laced with precipitated Barium, Strontium, and heavy metal scaling from produced water, pushes this ratio exceptionally high.

Scientific Basis: Studies of industrial mine tailings and legacy oilfield contamination confirm that heavy metal precipitation drastically increases SWIR reflectance while simultaneously dulling the visible green spectrum.3

Hydrocarbons (HCAI) 0-3M (B11 − B04) / (B11 + B04)

Mechanisms & Utility: The Hydrocarbon Absorption Index measures the disparity between standard SWIR reflectance and visible Red light absorption. Oil-contaminated earth absorbs red light at a substantially higher rate than natural iron-rich Permian soils.

Scientific Basis: Extensively documented in terrestrial oil spill tracking. Baseline red dirt registers at roughly ~0.25 on the HCAI scale; thick crude contamination spikes above 0.45.2

Salt Stress / Mortality (CRSI) 3-12M sqrt((B8×B4 − B3×B2) / (B8×B4 + B3×B2))

Mechanisms & Utility: The Canopy Response Salinity Index detects the physiological stress and osmotic shock inflicted by brine upon sparse surrounding vegetation (mesquite, grazing brush). Unlike NDSI which looks for bare salt crusts, CRSI reads the vegetation's internal response.

Scientific Basis: CRSI is deeply established in precision agriculture for tracking soil salinization. A sudden, sharp decline in CRSI surrounding a well pad is an irrefutable secondary indicator of off-pad liquid migration.6

4. Standard Environmental Monitors

Contaminated Soil / Clay Ratio (CSI) Persistent B11 / B12

Utility: Highly sensitive to clay minerals and soil disturbance. Useful for tracking pad construction, road grading, and identifying areas where protective topsoils have been stripped by fluid erosion.

Moisture Stress Index (MSI) Live B11 / B08

Utility: Effectively tracks drought stress. The SWIR band (B11) is absorbed by water, while the NIR band (B08) reflects healthy cell structure. As water depletes, B11 skyrockets relative to B08.7

Normalized Difference Moisture Index (NDMI) Live (B8A − B11) / (B8A + B11)

Utility: Calculates overall liquid water content in vegetation canopies. Useful for observing general site wetness and tracking the flow paths of both fresh and contaminated surface runoff.8

Vegetation Health (NDVI & SAVI) Persistent NDVI / Soil Adjusted Veg Index

Utility: NDVI is the industry absolute standard for measuring photosynthetic activity. SAVI applies a mathematical constant to correct for the high background reflectance of bare soil in arid environments like West Texas.9

Wetness / Ponding (NDWI) Live (B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11)

Utility: Specifically isolates free-standing water bodies. Imperative for ensuring containment ponds are accurately mapped and determining if fresh rainwater is skewing anomalous chemical alerts.10

Salinity Index (SI) 6M+ (B11 − B08) / (B11 + B08)

Utility: Tracks surface salt crusts (crystallized evaporites) on bare earth rather than mapping liquid brine. Extremely useful for verifying long-term historical damage scars from past blowouts.

SAR Physics (Sentinel-1) Persistent VV/VH Backscatter

Utility: Raw radar backscatter from Sentinel-1's C-band instrument. Bright areas equal rough terrain; dark areas represent smooth or wetted surfaces (capillary wave dampening). Radar effortlessly penetrates clouds and darkness for 24/7 intelligence acquisition.

False Color (NIR) Persistent RGB [B08, B04, B03]

Utility: Standard remote sensing composite replacing the Red channel with Near-Infrared. Vegetation glows bright red, making it exceptionally easy to visually isolate dead zones around active well pads.

True Color (TC) Persistent RGB [B04, B03, B02]

Utility: The standard optical spectrum as seen by the human eye. Used predominantly as a baseline truth layer before analyzing invisible or non-optical index wavelengths.
