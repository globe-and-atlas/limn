Experimental Spectral Screening Methods — Scientific status updated 2026-07-21

Limn combines established spectral indices with project-designed screening composites for inspecting produced-water-related surface context. The custom composites are research methods, not validated detectors; positive-looking pixels require independent source, field, and temporal checks.

Current scientific status

July 2026 controls found that the permissive pipeline paired PWCI/ASAI/OBEC recall of 81.5% / 77.8% / 66.7% with background activation of 96.7% / 71.3% / 71.3%. The shipped viewer activated on 0/150 background controls but was also blank at all 11 reviewed positive sites.

A 1,224-combination threshold sweep found no useful produced-water/caliche separation at the tested 500 m single-scene support. LBI activated at 2/4 standing-brine sites and 0/3 freshwater controls at the reviewed threshold, but the sample is not significant (two-sided Fisher exact p≈0.43) and does not establish brine specificity. These findings supersede older “validated,” “high-precision,” and “confirmed detection” wording elsewhere in historical notes.

Temporal Relevance Guide

Spectral signatures evolve as produced water interacts with the environment. Use the badges below to select the right tool for the age of the spill:

Live / 0-1M - Detects active wetting and dynamic moisture. 0-3M - Optimal window for liquid chemical signatures. 3-12M - Detects iron oxidation and early salt crusting. 6M+ - Forensic detection of legacy residues and mineral scabs. Persistent - Detects long-term soil/geological structural changes.

Permian Environmental Optimization (V40+)

The Permian Basin presents unique spectral challenges: high background reflectance from caliche soils, bright gypsum beds, and extremely low humidity. The following adjustments are hard-coded into the primary detection suites to suppress these natural "Project 0" false positives:

  • The "Liquid Gate" (+0.5 NDWI): Arid rangelands have a baseline NDWI near -0.6. We apply a +0.5 offset to lift "damp soil" signatures into the positive detection range without firing on bone-dry caliche.
  • BSI Mask Relaxation (-0.3): Standard soil masks (BSI) reject water bodies (negative BSI). Because severe hypersaline spills and standing oil appear spectrally "smooth" like water, we lowered the mask to -0.3 to prevent high-severity detections from being clipped.
  • SWIR Triple-Lock Offsets: PWCI has a permissive development configuration and a much stricter shipped-viewer configuration. Neither is a validated operating point: one activates on most background controls and the other suppresses both controls and reviewed positives.
  • Dry-surface path: The shipped ASAI viewer uses a dry path when NDWI < −0.42, NDSI > 0.15, and BSI > 0.52, with a 0.60 display floor. The current shipped OBEC evalscript does not contain the older documented dry path. Neither formula has demonstrated produced-water/caliche discrimination.

1. Experimental Produced-Water Screening Composites

The project-specific ASAI, PWCI, and OBEC architectures package established band ratios into reproducible multi-gate screening experiments. Their scientific contribution is the transparent implementation, verified-site evaluation, and bounded negative result—not a claim that the ratios retrieve produced-water chemistry or that literature priority has been established. Standard spectral indicators (NDVI, NDWI, NDSI, etc.) are in Section 3 with full citations.

ASAI — Arid Salinity Anomaly Index (formerly PWOI / APEX) ✧✧ Live Optical Smoothness × Moisture+Brine

The Gap: Identifying industrial spills in complex terrain produces "look-alike" false positives from shadows, bright soil, or dry salt ponds. No single-source optical index can reliably separate chemical moisture from physical surface texture across varied lighting and weather conditions.

The Implementation: ASAI (formerly PWOI) is the platform's broad-spectrum anomaly net. It uses a surface smoothness proxy — the ratio (B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11), an NDWI-like value that mirrors how specularly smooth liquid surfaces suppress SAR VH backscatter — cross-referenced against a combined moisture+brine score. A pixel is only flagged if it simultaneously exhibits optical smoothness and elevated chemical moisture, with a non-linear boost for high-confidence coincidence. Formerly known as Produced Water Optical Index (PWOI) or APEX Anomaly Index.41831

Dry Brine Mode: The wet-mode formula zeros on dry Permian Basin bare soil (NDWI = −0.39 to −0.51), which was the root cause of pre-fix 29.6% detection. A parallel dry path — (NDSI − 0.04) × min(1, BSI × N) × scale — fires when NDWI < −0.30 AND NDSI > 0.05 AND BSI > 0.10, recovering evaporated salt crust signatures. Score takes max(wet, dry) to preserve standing-water detections.

Current evaluation: The historical 77.8% development recall coincided with 71.3% background activation. The shipped viewer was blank at all 11 reviewed positives and all 150 controls. The dry path remains an implemented salt-crust screening hypothesis, not a validated spill detector.

ASAI vs. OBEC — When to Use Each

ASAI and OBEC produce visually similar outputs because both use the B03/B11 optical smoothness ratio in standard mode. The critical difference is in what chemical evidence they require before firing:

Practical workflow: Use ASAI and OBEC as contrasting anomaly views, then inspect time series, context imagery, records, and field or higher-resolution evidence. Agreement between the composites is not independent confirmation because they reuse bands and related surface proxies.

PWCI — Produced-Water Contrast Index (formerly PWI) ✧✧ 0-3M Restrictive Triple-Gate Engine

The Gap: Permian Basin caliche, bare pads, moisture, and vegetation can produce broad-band contrasts that resemble spill-related surface change. PWCI tests whether three such contrasts co-occur, but co-occurrence is not chemical identification.

The Implementation: PWCI (formerly PWI) is a restrictive "AND GATE" engine requiring the simultaneous elevation of three thresholded surface contrasts: dual-SWIR (NDSI legacy key), SWIR1–Red (HCAI legacy key), and SWIR2/Green (HMRI legacy key). The Limn implementation applies all three simultaneously as a produced-water screening hypothesis. The multiplicative gating means a strong signal on one component cannot compensate for a missing signal on another — all three must be present. A power scaling (pow(pwi_base × 50, 1.2)) stretches larger composite scores while suppressing marginal signals. Formerly known as Produced Water Index (PWI).1222

Threshold calibration note: The development pipeline lowered its exploratory thresholds after the original gate returned no positives. Those fitted thresholds are not literature-established chemical boundaries and are not the same as the precision-first thresholds in the interactive viewer.

Current evaluation: The 81.5% development recall coincided with 96.7% background activation. The shipped viewer was blank at all 11 reviewed positives and all 150 controls. PWCI is retained as a reproducible architecture and negative result, not a substantiated detector.

MVPI legacy — Single-Scene SWIR Ratio Screen Live Research-only surface proxy

The Gap: Methane ($CH_4$) super-emitter plumes from leaking oil pads, venting wellheads, and landfills are invisible to the naked eye. While dedicated spectrometers (like GHGSat or EMIT) track methane, high-frequency Sentinel-2 screening is historically underutilized due to caliche soil reflectance noise and false positives from water and vegetation.

The Implementation: This legacy experiment thresholds the single-scene B11 / B12 surface ratio and multiplies it by bright-ground, water-rejection, and vegetation-rejection gates. The map applies a final ×3 display scale. Soil, evaporites, moisture, illumination, and atmosphere can all alter the same ratio.

Scientific boundary: Established Sentinel-2 methane methods use scene fitting and commonly a methane-free reference observation. This layer implements neither MBSP nor MBMP and is not a methane retrieval, plume detector, or emissions estimate.36

2. Custom Composites & Applied Indices

These Limn composites combine established broad-band ratios into explicit, reproducible screening hypotheses. Their implementation is original to the project; their names do not establish chemical specificity, novelty in the literature, or detector validation.

OBEC — Optical Brightness/Edge Contrast (legacy HPWI) ✧ 0-3M PWCI × Smoothness (Optical / SAR)

Mechanisms & Utility: An experimental optical-contrast screening layer. In standard mode, OBEC (formerly HPWI) combines a blue/SWIR contrast (NDOI legacy key: (B02 − B12) / (B02 + B12)), a positive dual-SWIR term, and an NDWI-derived surface term ((B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11)) as a liquid-surface gate. In Deep Spectral Fusion mode, the optical proxy is not replaced by Sentinel-1 in the shipped app; the live formula remains Sentinel-2 only. Its broad bands do not retrieve an oil/brine emulsion or confirm produced water. Formerly known as Hybrid Produced Water Index (HPWI).41731

Implemented formula: clamp(6 × clamp(NDOI + 0.8·max(0,NDSI−τ),0,1) × clamp((NDWI+0.3)/0.6,0,1),0,1), displayed above 0.08. The shipped evalscript has no dry branch.

Current evaluation: Development recall was 66.7% with 71.3% background activation; the shipped viewer was blank at all reviewed positives and controls.

LBI — Liquid/Salinity Response Index ✧ 0-3M 20 × gates(NDSI, NDWI, low NDVI, BSI), with standing-water bypass

Mechanisms & Utility: Experimental water/salinity response layer. LBI combines a dual-SWIR contrast, wetness, low vegetation, and a surface gate; pixels with NDWI > 0.30 bypass the BSI term because open water drives BSI negative.

Calibration: The current render is intentionally stricter than the original broad LBI screen. It subtracts a small brine floor, uses a reduced Permian wetness offset, rejects vegetated pixels above the low-NDVI gate, and requires the bare/liquid surface proxy to stay above the desert background floor. Preliminary controls show low caliche activation but overlapping standing-brine and freshwater responses.

Scientific Basis: Built from established salinity and water-index literature, assembled as a project screening proxy. The current small sample does not establish brine specificity or produced-water accuracy.110

Vegetation/Dual-SWIR Stress Composite (VSI legacy) ✧ 3-24M dual-SWIR × red-edge term × MSI

Mechanisms & Utility: VSI combines a dual-SWIR contrast with red-edge and canopy-moisture terms. It can screen for co-located vegetation and surface response, but drought, grazing, phenology, disease, soil background, and many other stressors are confounded. Limn has not calibrated this composite to chloride, metals, time-to-wilting, or produced-water exposure.61520

SAR Surface-Contrast Index (SCRI legacy) ✧ 3M+ 0.6·VHdB + 0.4·(VH−VV)dB

The Gap: Optical surface contrasts can be unavailable under cloud, dust, or smoke; SAR provides a complementary surface-response view.

The Application: SCRI combines log-scaled Sentinel-1 VH backscatter with the VH−VV difference. It can expose surface-state changes under cloud cover, but roughness, moisture, geometry, vegetation, and substrate are confounded; it does not identify salt crust or estimate thickness.173031

Implementation note: The current formula uses log-scaled VH amplitude and a VH−VV ratio — standard SAR backscatter intensity analysis. It is not a full spatial texture measure (no GLCM or second-order statistics). The "roughness" claim is a backscatter-intensity proxy, not a true surface micro-topography measurement. A future version using windowed texture statistics would strengthen the physical justification.

Bare-Pad Three-Ratio Composite (BPI legacy) ✧ Live BSI × dual-SWIR × SWIR1–Red

Mechanisms & Utility: BPI requires a bare-surface response, positive dual-SWIR contrast, and positive SWIR1–Red contrast. It is useful for inspecting whether several surface signals co-locate on pads or roads, but does not identify brine, petroleum, or a leak.

Calibration boundary: The thresholds are project screening parameters, not chemical boundaries. Caliche, dry playas, disturbed soil, and moisture can all activate or suppress the terms.12

Three-Ratio Residue Composite (TRI legacy) ✧ 6M+ dual-SWIR × SWIR2/Green × Red/Blue–SWIR

Mechanisms & Utility: TRI multiplies three thresholded surface ratios to suppress pixels that respond in only one channel. It may support retrospective inspection of persistent surface change, but the ratios do not retrieve toxicity, metals, oxidation state, or spill age.

SWIR-Shoulder Surface Composite (PHI legacy) ✧ 0-6M dual-SWIR × (B11/B12) × SWIR1–Red

Mechanisms & Utility: PHI combines three correlated broad-band surface contrasts. It may highlight material or moisture differences, but Sentinel-2's bands do not make this an oil classifier or oily-brine separator.213

Clay/Surface Contrast Composite (CMA legacy) ✧ Persistent NDSI × (B11/B12) × (B04/B02)

Mechanisms & Utility: CMA combines dual-SWIR, SWIR1/SWIR2, and Red/Blue terms as a persistent surface-context screen. These broad ratios respond to mineralogy, iron-rich soil, brightness, moisture, and disturbance; they do not demonstrate clay-lattice alteration or produced-water causation.11127

Green–SWIR Interaction Composite (HMI legacy) ✧ 12M+ (B03/B02) × (B11/B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: HMI multiplies thresholded Green/Blue and SWIR1/SWIR2 ratios. It can expose co-located visible/SWIR surface contrast, but without field chemistry and a calibrated model it cannot identify barium, strontium, or any other metal.325

Three-Channel Surface Context Composite (EHC legacy) ✧ 0-3M RGB [Blue–SWIR2, BSI, dual-SWIR]

Mechanisms & Utility: EHC maps three broad surface contrasts into RGB so spatial patterns can be inspected together. Its colors are not material classes: red does not prove oil, blue does not prove salt, and a halo pattern does not prove a blowout.141926

Red/Blue × SWIR Surface Contrast (AOI legacy) ✧ 0-6M (B04/B02) × (B11/B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: AOI multiplies Red/Blue and SWIR1/SWIR2 ratios. Iron-rich soil, mineralogy, moisture, vegetation, and illumination can alter the result. It does not retrieve oxygen state, iron chemistry, or produced-water causation.112227

Red/Blue–Dual-SWIR–Low-Vegetation Composite (FBC legacy) ✧ 3-12M 150·(ironScore·dualSWIRScore·noVeg)1.4

Mechanisms & Utility: FBC requires elevated Red/Blue contrast, positive dual-SWIR contrast, and low vegetation. The combination can help inspect co-occurring surface alteration, but it does not identify ferric iron, brine chemistry, or a spill.

Permian-Calibrated Gate Logic:

  • Gate 1 — Red/Blue contrast: ironScore = max(0, B04/B02 − τ)
  • Gate 2 — Dual-SWIR contrast: dualSWIRScore = max(0, NDSI − τ)
  • Gate 3 — No Vegetation: noVeg = max(0, 1 − max(0, NDVI))
    Suppresses any pixel with living plant cover.

Composite & Scaling: FBC = clamp(150 × (ironScore × dualSWIRScore × noVeg)1.4, 0, 1)
This is the exact current display mapping; it is not a probability.

Scientific boundary: Red/Blue and SWIR contrasts are broad surface responses. Mineral attribution requires independent spectroscopy, field observations, or laboratory analysis.11

Red-Edge/Dual-SWIR Alteration Composite (REAI) ✧ 3-12M (B06/B05) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: REAI multiplies a thresholded B06/B05 ratio by a thresholded dual-SWIR contrast. Vegetation structure, soil, moisture, geometry, and mineralogy are confounded. The composite does not confirm goethite, hematite, iron precipitation, or brine chemistry.11

Vegetation-Stress/Dual-SWIR Composite (VCBI legacy) ✧ 6-24M max(0, −CRSI) × NDSI

Mechanisms & Utility: VCBI combines the inverted CRSI display with positive dual-SWIR contrast. It can screen for co-located canopy and surface change, but does not confirm chloride exposure, brine migration, or causation. Phenology, drought, grazing, disease, soil background, and land management remain competing explanations.61

Prior Art & Original Contribution: Honest vulnerability claim (Medium-High strength). The original contribution is HSAI as an optical heat-shelter absence score for shade, canopy, surface dryness, imperviousness, and park-access context. Prior art includes albedo and canopy drought indexes. This composite does not assert retrieved air or surface temperature, heat index, or thermal remote sensing from Sentinel-2. The honesty makes it stronger: it claims vulnerability and shelter absence, not heat measurement.

3. Legacy Surface-Contrast Components

Dual-SWIR Contrast (NDTI/NBR2 form; NDSI legacy) 0-12M (B11 − B12) / (B11 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Normalized SWIR1–SWIR2 contrast. The same algebra is used as NDTI/NBR2 for tillage, residue, burn, moisture, and other surface-condition studies.

Scientific boundary: Retained as a salinity-hypothesis component, but it is not brine-specific and does not retrieve salt concentration.1

Blue–SWIR2 Contrast (NDOI legacy) 0-3M (B02 − B12) / (B02 + B12)

Mechanisms & Utility: Normalized Blue–SWIR2 surface contrast. Water, bright soil, shadow, aerosol, and material differences can all affect it.

Scientific boundary: A positive or negative response is not uniquely attributable to oil.5

SWIR2/Green Contrast (HMRI legacy) 12M+ B12 / B03

Mechanisms & Utility: Broad SWIR2/Green surface ratio sensitive to brightness, vegetation, moisture, substrate, and mineralogy.

Scientific boundary: Heavy-metal estimation requires field concentrations and calibrated multivariate models; this ratio cannot identify barium, strontium, radium, or total metal concentration.3

SWIR1–Red Contrast (HCAI legacy) 0-3M (B11 − B04) / (B11 + B04)

Mechanisms & Utility: Normalized SWIR1–Red surface contrast, confounded by soil color, iron oxides, vegetation, moisture, and illumination.

Scientific boundary: Broad Sentinel-2 bands do not reproduce the narrow hyperspectral Hydrocarbon Index absorption retrieval; treat this as context, not petroleum measurement.2

Inverted Canopy Response Index (1 − CRSI) 3-12M 1 − clamp(sqrt((B8×B4 − B3×B2) / (B8×B4 + B3×B2)),0,1)

Mechanisms & Utility: Limn displays the inverse of the established CRSI form so higher values represent lower CRSI response.

Scientific boundary: Water status, phenology, soil background, and many non-saline stressors can alter the response; it is not an irrefutable indicator of brine migration.6

4. Standard Environmental Monitors

SWIR1/SWIR2 Surface Ratio (CSI legacy) Persistent B11 / B12

Utility: Highly sensitive to clay minerals and soil disturbance. Useful for tracking pad construction, road grading, and identifying areas where protective topsoils have been stripped by fluid erosion.

Moisture Stress Index (MSI) Live B11 / B08

Utility: Effectively tracks drought stress. The SWIR band (B11) is absorbed by water, while the NIR band (B08) reflects healthy cell structure. As water depletes, B11 skyrockets relative to B08.7

Normalized Difference Moisture Index (NDMI) Live (B8A − B11) / (B8A + B11)

Utility: Calculates overall liquid water content in vegetation canopies. Useful for observing canopy and near-surface moisture response. It does not distinguish fresh, saline, or produced water.8

Vegetation Health (NDVI & SAVI) Persistent NDVI / Soil Adjusted Veg Index

Utility: NDVI is the industry absolute standard for measuring photosynthetic activity. SAVI applies a mathematical constant to correct for the high background reflectance of bare soil in arid environments like West Texas.9

Wetness / Ponding (NDWI) Live (B03 − B11) / (B03 + B11)

Utility: Xu-form MNDWI highlights open water and wet-surface contrast. Use it to check whether a candidate response follows a pond or drainage feature; it does not identify water chemistry or source.10

Automated Water Extraction Index — Shadow Variant (AWEIsh) Live B02 + 2.5·B03 − 1.5·(B08+B11) − 0.25·B12

Utility: Established water-extraction cross-check with a different response to shadow and bright surfaces than MNDWI. Agreement supports water-like surface context only—not salinity, brine, or produced-water attribution.21

Normalized Difference Red-Edge Index (NDRE) Persistent (B8A − B05) / (B8A + B05)

Utility: Complements NDVI and SAVI for vegetation-response inspection. Phenology, drought, disease, grazing, fire, and management are confounders, so stress cannot be assigned to produced water without independent temporal and field evidence.

Bare Soil Index (BSI) Persistent ((B11+B04)−(B08+B02)) / ((B11+B04)+(B08+B02))

Utility: Combines SWIR, Red, NIR, and Blue bands to distinguish exposed ground from vegetated and wetted surfaces. High BSI is expected on ordinary caliche, pads, roads, and disturbed soil; it is context, not confirmation of a spill footprint.

Scientific Basis: BSI gates PWCI's bare-surface response and ASAI's dry-surface path. Its high natural Permian background is also a major reason the experimental composites fail to separate spill sites from controls.

Salinity Index (SI) 6M+ (B11 − B08) / (B11 + B08)

Utility: Broad SWIR1–NIR surface contrast historically labeled as a salinity index. It can support locally calibrated salt-affected-soil studies but does not verify evaporites, blowout damage, or salt concentration in Limn.

SAR Physics (Sentinel-1) Persistent VV/VH Backscatter

Utility: Raw radar backscatter from Sentinel-1's C-band instrument. Bright areas equal rough terrain; dark areas represent smooth or wetted surfaces (capillary wave dampening). Radar effortlessly penetrates clouds and darkness for 24/7 intelligence acquisition.

False Color (NIR) Persistent RGB [B08, B04, B03]

Utility: Standard remote sensing composite replacing the Red channel with Near-Infrared. Vegetation glows bright red, making it exceptionally easy to visually isolate dead zones around active well pads.

SWIR Surface Context Persistent RGB [B12, B11, B04]

Utility: False-color inspection of broad wetness, vegetation, bare-ground, pad, and substrate differences. The colors are not material classes, salinity estimates, or contamination labels.

True Color (TC) Persistent RGB [B04, B03, B02]

Utility: The required first-pass visual context for inspecting pads, ponds, roads, shadows, disturbance, and obvious scene problems before any index response is interpreted.
