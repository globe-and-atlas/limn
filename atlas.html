How Atlas coverage works

          

            Atlas catalogs 91 novel spectral indices across 12 environmental
            domains, plus live Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-5P air-quality
            demonstrators. Each index was designed for the sensors best suited to its
            phenomenon — so not all map live here. Atlas streams tiles from public Copernicus WMS
            endpoints: Sentinel-2 L2A (optical, 13 bands), Sentinel-1
            GRD (radar, all-weather), and Sentinel-5P TROPOMI
            (atmospheric trace gases). Indices computable from those map live; the rest stay in
            the catalog as documented concepts so the full body of research remains visible.
          


          
Of the 91 novel catalog


          

            

              
              —
              Live-mappable
            

            
Novel indices that render live from Copernicus — Sentinel-2 optical, plus the two
              that graduated to radar/atmospheric (FGDCI on Sentinel-1, SACI on Sentinel-5P) —
              each paired with a validated peak-signal bookmark. Click one and Atlas flies to the
              place and date where the signal is unmistakable.

          


          

            

              
              —
              Sentinel-2-capable · proof pending
            

            
The formula runs on Sentinel-2, but a location and date where the signal is
              unmistakable hasn't been locked in yet. Shown as True Color context until a proof
              target is validated — then they graduate to live-mappable.

          


          

            

              
              —
              Sensor-limited
            

            
These need an instrument the public Sentinel-2 service can't provide:
              hyperspectral imagers (EMIT, EnMAP, PACE, PRISMA, DESIS), thermal (ECOSTRESS,
              Landsat TIRS), atmospheric trace-gas (TROPOMI, GOES), SAR / lidar / gravimetry
              (Sentinel-1, NISAR, ICESat-2, GRACE-FO), or commercial high-res (Planet). They're
              concept entries — Atlas shows True Color context at a representative location.

          


          
What the T1 / T2 / T3 badges mean

          
Every novel index carries a novelty badge — how new the
            formula is, independent of whether it maps live:


          

            T1
            
First formula  —
              the detection purpose exists, but no named, standardized formula had been published.
              Atlas proposes the first one to use, adapt, and build on.

          

          

            T2
            
First standardized formula  —
              the concept appears in the literature only as a qualitative description. No named
              formula with defined inputs had been written down. Atlas writes it out.

          

          

            T3
            
Newly computable  —
              the spectral physics was published years ago, but the sensor needed to compute it
              only launched 2019–2024 (EMIT, PACE, EnMAP, NISAR). Operational for the first time.

          


          
Separate from the 91


          

            

              
              —
              Live sensor demonstrators
            

            
Not novel indices — established, single-scene techniques (radar water/urban/
              vegetation; NO₂ and SO₂ retrievals) added purely to show the Sentinel-1 and
              Sentinel-5P pipelines work. Badged DEMO and grouped in their own
              "· Live" domains, so the novel total stays exactly 91.

          


          

            All figures are computed live from the catalog: the three tiers above always sum to
            the 91 novel indices, and demonstrators are tallied apart.
